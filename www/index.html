SHSAT admissions by middle school for 2018-19

This table displays offers of admission to eighth graders at each New York City public middle school for the 2018-19 school year to the eight New York City high schools that decide admissions exclusively based on the Specialized High School Admissions Test (SHSAT): Stuyvesant, Bronx Science; Brooklyn Tech, Staten Island Tech, High School of American Studies, High School for Math, Science and Engineering, Queens Science High School and Brooklyn Latin. In total, students received about 5,000 offers for approximately 3,800 seats.

Mayor de Blasio proposes to eliminate the SHSAT and offer admission to the top 7% academic performers among eighth graders at each middle school, based on class grades and standardized tests. A bill in the state Legislature to implement his plan would restrict admission to those who are in the 25% of students citywide, and enable students not in the top 7% but with a 3.7 minimum GPA (as well as students attending private middle school or moving in from outside NYC) to receive offers via lottery.

For many middle schools, automatic admission of the top 7% of students would result in significant change in the number of students who receive offers to a specialized high school – the difference between the number of students in the “accepted” column and those in the “top 7%” column. (Note: the table does not reflect the impact of limiting offers to the top 25% of students citywide or of additional seats that students may be offered via lottery.)
